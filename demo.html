Common Investor Questions
"How is this different from public charging networks?" +
Public networks are expensive (65-85p/kWh vs our 18-32p), require detours, and are often broken. We're not competing with them — we're serving the 60% of EV owners they completely ignore: renters and flat dwellers who need convenient, affordable, near-home charging.
"What stops someone just using ChargePoint or Pod Point?" +
Those are public network operators. Our hosts are private individuals. We're peer-to-peer, like Airbnb vs a hotel. The host can be 50 metres from the driver's front door. No other platform does hyperlocal residential charging.
"What's stopping a big company copying this?" +
Network effects. The value of NestCharge is the density of hosts in each neighbourhood. Once we have 20 hosts in a postcode, drivers always have somewhere nearby. That density is hard to replicate quickly — and it compounds as drivers bring more hosts.
"What about insurance and liability?" +
We've designed around this. Hosts use their existing home insurance (which typically covers occasional commercial activity). We provide session-level liability coverage for any charging-related incidents as part of our platform fee. We've modelled this on how Airbnb approached host protection.
"This is early stage — where's the revenue?" +
We're pre-revenue by design — we're building supply (hosts) before opening demand (drivers) to avoid the empty marketplace problem. The waitlist proves demand exists. The £350K seed specifically funds the supply-side onboarding to create our first dense neighbourhood in London.

List your charger
